The Mod One Experience

My first time around I spent too much time worrying over things in my personal life
and not enough time focusing on what I needed to do. And on top of that I kept trying to compare
my progress with people who were ahead of me and took it personally. Going through again,
without being distracted, helped me realize that the only thing preventing me from passing
was putting in the time.

Realizing that really helped me focus on what I needed to do the second time around.
Because not everyone got the opportunity to take this challenge and I'm sure a lot of people
more qualified than myself were turned down. But it really hit home for me that if you really
want something you have to be willing to put in the time. It will never just come to you. You cannot
look at the person next to you and think about how it's easy for them and not you.

That may even be partially true. But, everyone could get there with the time and effort.


Alsmost as a side note: TDD was my favorite technical concept. It really helps, especially
early on for learing how to work through your errors to come to your solution. And I will try
to take that practice with me where ever I go.
